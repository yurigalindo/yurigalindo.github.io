Yuri Galindo

I am performing research through the Fatima Fellowship, advised by Vaishnavh Nagarajan. I am also a computer vision scientist at Itaú Unibanco, the largest bank of Latin America.

I want to make computer vision models more robust. Although models are able to achieve astounding performance at benchmarks, they fail in the presence of imperceptible pixel changes or small domain shifts. I studied robustness in the context of CLIP, and am currently looking at this problem through the lens of simplicity bias. My goal is to deepen our understanding of robustness, and use this to build more robust models.

I studied engineering at the Technological Institute of Aeronautics, and obtained my B.S. in computer science at the Federal University of São Paulo. During my studies, I managed to publish 3 papers and obtain my full-time position at Itaú.
